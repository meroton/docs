Meroton visited BazelCon 2023 in Munich October 24-25, 2023.
During the conference, we held three talks:
* [Remote Output Service - How not to have your bytes and eat them too](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Dc8R_Zrf6M#t=7h36m20s)
  by Benjamin Ingberg about bb_clientd and the Remote Output Service.
* [Buildbarn - From 100 to 100.000 CPUs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IXimf4DCAoY#t=3h2m10s)
  ([slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/14qgMa1J9hBMTU017PlpQokKtS5n0KxJbJXPjndxqPqE/edit?usp=sharing&resourcekey=0-OF3Hrwvr5G7aDnJG_ABRhw))
  by Fredrik Medley.
* [Dude, where is my RAM? - An adventure in finding a RAM thief in Starlak land](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IXimf4DCAoY#t=7h21m57s)
  by Nils Wireklint about difficulties in debugging out-of-memory errors in Bazel.

Other talks that mentioned Buildbarn were:
* [Migrating a Multiple-Platform Game Engine to Bazel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Dc8R_Zrf6M#t=5h41m5s)
  where Kai Zhang from NetEase talks about a
  [Buildbarn worker implementation in Python for Windows and MacOS](https://github.com/kkpattern/bb-remote-execution-py).
* [Planting Bazel in barren soil: A Perl Story](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Dc8R_Zrf6M#t=7h56m2s)
  where Manuel Naranjo from Booking.com is using Buildbarn for remote execution and Buildbuddy for Build Event Streaming.

We are thankful for all amazing chats with the community and are looking forward to BazelCon 2024.
